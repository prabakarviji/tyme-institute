Born from Love. Built for Change

Tyler Mert Ergisi was the kind of soul you never forget. Full of life, radiating joy, wit, curiosity, and compassion—he had an extraordinary gift for making others feel seen and valued.

He loved technology, music, good food, late-night conversations, and connecting with everyone around him, young and old alike.

At just 16, Tyler was diagnosed with osteosarcoma—a rare and aggressive bone cancer. In an instant, everything changed. But Tyler met this challenge the same way he met life: with unwavering courage, hope, and a strength far beyond his years.

For three years, Tyler fought bravely through surgeries, chemotherapy, clinical trials, and long hospital stays. Even when he lost his ability to walk, he never lost his light.

His battle wasn’t just against cancer—it was for his future and for every child facing the same reality.

We lost Tyler at 19. But his legacy had only just begun.

Through Tyler’s journey, we witnessed the heartbreaking truths about pediatric cancer: underfunded research, outdated treatments, and far too few options for children and teens. We saw young lives shaped by pain when they should have been full of discovery and dreams.

We couldn’t stay still. We couldn’t stay silent.

In his memory, we founded the TyMe Institute—a movement dedicated to changing the future of cancer through prevention, early detection, and research that prioritizes lives before cancer takes hold. This is our way of honoring Tyler’s strength and belief that even a short life can be lived with great purpose.

Providing personalized healthcare

We focus on personalized care to meet your unique needs, combining innovation and compassion for a truly patient-centered approach.

We’re embracing groundbreaking technology and the power of artificial intelligence to bring the science of cancer prevention to the world. Our mission is to end the fight against cancer — one individual at a time — and pave the way toward a healthier, brighter future for all.

Our mission is to promote early detection of cancer through accessible, equitable, and technology-driven screening programs.We strive to educate communities on the importance of regular cancer screening to improve health outcomes and save lives.Our organization is committed to reducing cancer disparities by reaching underserved populations with culturally competent care.Through collaboration, innovation, and advocacy, we aim to create a future where cancer is detected early and treated effectively.
